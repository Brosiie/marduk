# The Story of Marduk

## The Cycle

Before time, there was salt water and sweet water. The sweet water was Apsu, the salt water was Tiamat, and from their mingling came every god. Apsu grew tired of the noise of his children and tried to silence them. Ea killed him first.

Tiamat made an army from her grief. Eleven monsters, born of her flesh, captained by the demon Kingu whose chest she set with the Tablet of Destinies. None of the gods would face her. They cowered.

Then Marduk stood up.

He demanded the kingship of heaven if he won. They gave it. He took the four winds in nets and the seven storms on a chain, climbed his chariot of red flame, and met Tiamat in single combat. He drove the winds into her open mouth so she could not close it, then split her in two with an arrow. From her body he made the sky. From her eyes he made the Twin Rivers (Idiglat and Buranum, what later mortals call Tigris and Euphrates). From her ribs he made the cradle of the world.

Then he wrote his Edict on the Iron Pillar at the heart of Babilim, and set seven seals on the gates of the underworld where Tiamat's blood still stirred.

That was the First Cycle. Mortals were born and lived and died and built and forgot. The seals held for seventeen hundred years.

The seals are failing now.

## The Six Champions

When the first seal cracked, six children were born under the same broken star. None knew the others. Each was raised differently. Each met fire and survived. Each begins the game on the worst day of their life.

### Berserker - The Last of Ash-Step
**Intro zone:** The Ash-Step Camp.
**Mini-boss:** Raid Captain Hassu, called the Hooked.

The Ash-Step clans range the dry plains north of Reed Wastes. They are tattooed for grief, and a Berserker is tattooed every time they kill. Your village had eighty-seven souls. The raiders came in the night with hooked spears. The Hooked himself drove a spear through your father's chest while you watched from under the cattle pen. You killed the man pinning your mother by biting his throat out. You were six.

You spent twenty-two years training. Now you have come back. The raiders' camp is on the same hill, smaller now, the smoke thinner. The Hooked is still there. He does not remember you. He will.

### Assassin - The Master's Lie
**Intro zone:** The Whisper Shrine.
**Mini-boss:** Master Sapum, the Five-Mouthed.

The Whisper Shrine took you in at four. You learned to walk on rope, fall on stone, sleep three days a week. You loved Master Sapum. You called him father in the secret way the shrine permits. He knew it.

Then the seal under the shrine cracked. Master Sapum heard a voice through it. The voice promised him five mouths to speak with, one for each god of the underworld. He listened. He said yes. He fed his disciples to the seal one by one to thicken his power.

You killed the brother he sent for you in the laundry. You took a knife. You went up the seven flights to find the man you called father. He has five mouths now. He still smiles at you with all of them.

### Ronin - Sword Without Lord
**Intro zone:** The Sword-Vow Ruins.
**Mini-boss:** Enforcer Kazat, the Iron-Faced.

You served Lord Ennum for nine years. You rose from squire to senior blade. He was a good man and a bad strategist. His brother Lord Tashmu was the reverse. The coup was inevitable; everyone but Ennum saw it coming.

The night the gates fell you were on the wall. By the time you reached the throne hall Tashmu's enforcer Kazat had Ennum on his knees, sword already raised. Ennum looked at you, smiled the polite smile he used for foreign ambassadors, and said *go. Live. Find another path.* Then he died.

You ran. You hid in a temple of breathing. The monks let you stay. They taught you the seven breaths because you would not stop crying. Now four years later you are coming back. Kazat is on the wall.

### Ranger - The Spawn That Came Through
**Intro zone:** The Greenheart Glade.
**Mini-boss:** The Glade Terror (an unnamed Tiamat-spawn beast).

You grew up in a forest village called Eshpaq, sixty souls under the canopy of the Greenheart. Hunters' children. You learned to read tracks at three.

A seal cracked under the Greenheart in spring. Something came up through the loam. It was not a wolf, not a bear, not a thing with a name. It ate forty-one of your kin in one night. You found your uncle in two pieces near the river. Your sister's bow you have not found, though you have found her quiver.

You are tracking it now. The trail is wide and stinks of iron. It does not know you are following. You hope.

### Mage - Pages and Ash
**Intro zone:** The Inkstone Tower.
**Mini-boss:** The Tower Warden (a binding-construct gone wrong).

The Inkstone Tower is the lesser of the three arcane libraries of Babilim. Yours was the third floor, Bindings and Cosmographies. Your master was Old Asaridu, who taught you that magic is etiquette: every word in the world has a name, and the trick is to ask correctly.

A seal failed under the foundation. Something came up; the Warden, the Tower's living guardian, was bound around it but the binding cracked too. Old Asaridu sealed himself into the central well to hold the breach. The Warden patrols what is left.

You are climbing the tower with your master's books in a satchel and a single binding-rod he left under your pillow. Seven floors. The Warden is on the seventh.

### Paladin - The Chapel Stood / The Chapel Wept
**Intro zone:** The Sun-Sworn Chapel.
**Mini-boss:** Siege-Master Beleti.

The Sun-Sworn Chapel is older than Babilim. It sits on a hill west of Ashurim, dedicated to Marduk's Light. You woke up there, in the crypt, with a shield on your back and a hammer at your knee. You don't remember the night before.

You can hear the siege from inside the crypt. Boots on the chapel floor. Someone screaming in the apse. Someone praying in the nave. You go up.

In the nave the Siege-Master is killing the wounded. In the apse the chapel-master is healing the dying.

You choose where to stand.

If you walk to the nave first, you become a **Guardian**. Plate, shield, war-hammer. Your job is to absorb. The siege-master will not pass.

If you walk to the apse first, you become a **Lightbringer**. Mail, shield, ceremonial mace. Your job is to keep them alive. The chapel-master will not fall.

Either way, the siege-master Beleti dies tonight. The chapel-master either lives or doesn't. You learn very early that you cannot be in both places at once.

### Chaos Druid - The Coven Burned
**Intro zone:** The Coven Glen.
**Mini-boss:** Inquisitor Prime Sahirum, called Witch-Burner.

Your mother was a hedge-druid. Your father was something else. You have always been able to feel weather a day before it arrived. You once walked into a circle of birch trees as a girl and walked out a fox; you remember the taste of mouse very clearly.

The covenant of Old Druids found you at twelve. They said you carried the blood of Tiamat-touched, that the chaos in your line was older than Marduk's edict and would wake whether you wanted or not. They taught you anyway. They were kind.

The Witch-Burner came in the autumn. The Inquisition has hunted Tiamat-blooded since the Edict; few survive being found. Sahirum killed your teachers in front of you. You burst out of yourself, became something with too many teeth, and ran. The Glen still smolders.

The chaos in you is awake now. You go back to the Glen because Sahirum is still hunting. He thinks you are dead. Disabuse him.

## Convergence: Ashurim

After your mini-boss, you arrive at Ashurim.

It is a market town on the road to Babilim, named for Anshar (god of the heavens, father of Marduk's father). It has one inn, the Singing Goat. The inn-keep is a woman called Belitu who is older than she looks. The town has one Storyteller, and the Storyteller knows your name before you give it.

The other five champions are here too. Some arrived earlier, some later. They watch each other warily across the common-room fire. The Storyteller stands up. She is small, and old, and her eyes are gold.

She says: *seven seals. Six of you. The seventh is still walking.*

This is where the prologue ends and the game proper begins. From Ashurim you can travel to:
- Iron Crown Outskirts (lvl 6-12)
- Reed Wastes (lvl 10-20)
- Babilim (the city itself, all-level hub)

## Babilim, the Iron Crown

Capital of the world. Built on the spine of the cradle Marduk made from Tiamat's ribs.

**Districts:**
- **The Iron Pillar District** - center of the city, holds the Edict pillar itself. Council seat. Royal architecture.
- **The Hanging Gardens** - tiered terraces of vegetation fed from the Twin Rivers; ranger contact, beast vendor, herbalist.
- **The Six Gates** - each named for a god (Anshar, Kishar, Ea, Nergal, Adad, Lahmu). Six points of egress to the regions.
- **The Lapis Quarter** - imported goods, sea-trader district, fence's market for stolen Tiamat-spawn parts.
- **The Crooked Stair** - the temple district. Dojos representing Water, Stone, Flame, Wind, Mist, Thunder maintain liaison branches here. Sun Breathing has no liaison; nobody knows it survives.
- **The Bone Quarter** - graveyards and the offices of the Inquisition (yes, they hunt the Druids; yes, you can see them every day). Cleric vendors.
- **The Thieves' Kitchen** - undercity, assassin guild contact, black market.
- **The Silent Gate** - the seventh gate, the one Marduk shut. Seal cracking visibly; do not go near it before level 60.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Belitu the Innkeeper** of the Singing Goat (Ashurim) - main quest giver until level 30.
- **The Storyteller** - moves between Ashurim, Babilim, and the Ascension Plane. Knows things.
- **High Magus Iddinu** - head of the arcane council, condescending to non-mages, useful.
- **General Sin-Mushezib** - Crown's military commander, hires for Reed Wastes campaigns.
- **The Oracle of the Pillar** - blind, mute, writes prophecies in chalk on the Edict stones; legitimate, not a fraud.
- **Black-Sail** - leader of the Pirate Isles; not at Babilim normally but visits at festivals.
- **Sahirum the Witch-Burner** - Inquisition Prime. Recurring antagonist for Druid players. Survives mini-boss kill if Druid has `inquisitor_prime_killed` not set.

## Faction Map

**Crown of Babilim** - the empire. Lawful order, Edict-orthodox. Quest-giver hub.

**The Inquisition** - witch-hunters. Hostile to Druids by default, neutral to others. Some nuanced moments: an Inquisitor can be a friend if you are a Mage with a clean record.

**The Old Druids** - reduced to scattered covens. Friendly to Druids, suspicious of others.

**The Six Breaths** - the six legitimate breathing schools (Water, Flame, Stone, Wind, Mist, Thunder). Loose alliance, not unified. Each has its own dojo, vendors, quest line, master to train under.

**The Black Sail** - pirate consortium. Contracts available, faction reputation independent of Crown.

**The Spawn-Eaters** - cult that worships Tiamat as the wronged mother. Hostile by default. Some sympathetic NPCs in their ranks.

**The Sun-Sworn** - hidden cult that knows Sun Breathing did not die with the Bloodline. Contact only after defeating Tiamat.

## The Three Big Bads

**Kingu the Tablet-Bearer** (mid-game major villain, lvl ~50). Tiamat's old champion, freed from his binding when seals failed. Roams the Sundered Coast. Drops tableted shards.

**Tiamat** (climactic boss, lvl ~80). Three phases: Drowned (water), Risen (lightning), True (mother-of-monsters dragon form). Arena: top tier of the Black Citadel, open sky.

**Lucifer** (secret post-Tiamat, lvl ~95). Polite, charming, fully willing to negotiate. Three phases: Diplomat (talking encounter, choices matter for dialogue), Ember (fire and bargain), Fallen (true form). Arena: bottom of the Fire Stair. Defeating him sets `lucifer_defeated` (run) and `demon_class_unlocked` (permanent). The Demon class menu opens for any future character on this save profile.

## Mob Roster, Per Region

A careful adventurer who reads the codex entries learns more lore from the mobs than from the books. Each mob is a small story.

### The Cradle (intro zones)

- **Ash-Step Raider:** "tattooed for grief," carries a hooked spear and a list of grievances.
- **Whisper Acolyte:** first-year initiate of the Whisper Shrine. Hasn't yet realized Sapum is feeding the shrine to the seal.
- **Tashmu's Footman:** conscripted from farms Lord Ennum used to visit. Some still remember kindness.
- **Tiamat-Touched Wolf:** was a wolf, before the seal cracked. Now there are too many teeth.
- **Lesser Binding-Construct:** what was meant to keep the seal shut. The seal is open. They still patrol.
- **Inquisition Blood-Hunter:** drains witch-blood into rune-jars. Carries six on his belt.
- **Siege Lieutenant:** reports to Beleti. Was a Crown soldier until the schism.

### The Reed Wastes

- **Reed-Creeper:** crawls on its belly. The reeds remember being marsh.
- **Salt-Demon:** Tiamat's blood salted the wastes. Some of the salt got up.
- **Wastes-Walker:** Ash-Step pilgrim who got lost in the cracking. The reeds did not let him leave.
- **Thirst-Spirit:** ghost from before the marsh dried. Still looking for the well it remembers.

### Lapis Bay

- **Black-Sail Crew:** lesser sailors of the three pirate kings.
- **Salt-Witch:** discharged Lapis Bay water-mage who took a pirate contract. Pays better.
- **Drowned Sailor:** Lapis Bay's old shipwrecks send up walking dead at low tide.
- **Lapis Python:** from before the bay was a bay. Bigger than it should be. Patient.

### Bone Mountains

- **Ossuary Guardian:** made from the bones of the people who built the charnel-roads.
- **Bone-Swarm:** loose bones that found each other. Doing teamwork.
- **Stone Priest:** senior monk who refused to die when his stance broke.
- **Echo of the First Climber:** the first surveyor died here. Some of him keeps trying to finish.

### Verdant Wound

- **Thorn-Walker:** what the thorn bushes became when blood reached the roots.
- **Wound-Dryad:** was a dryad. Still is, technically. The wood is wrong.
- **Twelve-Clawed:** crawls. Sings the Greenheart's old songs wrong. Knows your name.
- **Sanctum Apprentice (Lost):** Druid student who walked too far. Came back wearing more skin.

### Ember Steppes

- **Steppe Salamander:** lives in the fissures. Larger than a horse.
- **Flame Apostate:** Flame Breathing senior who let his inner flame go out and replaced it.
- **Steppe Bandit:** rides salamanders. Has a rule about children. Refuses to discuss it.
- **Ember Imp:** small fire-spirit attached to the Pillar of Nergal.

### Mist Vale

- **Walker in the Fog at Dusk:** the figure you've always seen at dusk. Wearing the neighbor's face.
- **Mist Apostate:** Mist Breathing monk who walked too far in.
- **Pilgrim's Echo:** pilgrims came here to be forgiven. Some stay.

### Shrieking Highlands

- **Thunder-Apostate:** walked into the storm to die. Did not. Adad is embarrassed.
- **Storm-Rider:** used to ride horses. Now rides bolts.
- **Adad's Drone:** bee-shaped lightning elemental. Stings only insults.

### Sundered Coast

- **Tiamat-Spawn Brood:** hatched from one of the half-sunken ribs. Small but never alone.
- **Tiamat's Deep Servant:** older than the binding.
- **Sundered Priest:** surviving cultist of the Spawn-Eaters.

### Black Citadel

- **Bound Spawn:** permanently bound to the Citadel's walls. Hasn't eaten in 400 years.
- **Citadel Priest:** spawn-eater promoted. Wears Crown vestments under spawn-skin.
- **Tier-Warden:** former people. Now paperwork that bites.

### Fire Stair

- **Servant of the Fall:** demon Lucifer keeps. Used to him.
- **Ember Drake:** small dragon. Lucifer feeds them off-hand. Loyal as dogs.
- **Silver Negotiator:** lesser diplomats. Will offer you the same deal in less polished words.

## Landmarks

35+ Points of Interest scattered through the world. Examining one rewards XP and progresses Explorer achievements. Notable:

- **The Iron Pillar:** Marduk's edict in nickel-iron. Hums when you stand near. Hum changes pitch when you speak.
- **The Silent Gate:** the seventh gate of Babilim, the one Marduk shut. Hairline crack near the keystone. Listen and you hear nothing, but you feel something listening back.
- **Tiamat's Edge:** salt-eaten pillar at Lapis Bay's high-tide line. "HERE THE SEA WAS THE GODDESS."
- **The First Step:** Mist Vale entrance stone. Carved with names of pilgrims who entered and did not leave. Many. Recent.
- **Tiamat's Throne:** her skull. The throne is her skull. The skull is the throne. You can sit on it before the fight; nobody will stop you.
- **Asaridu's Well:** Old Asaridu sealed himself in here. His voice still speaks at noon, if you remember his student-name.

Full registry in `scripts/landmarks/landmark_registry.gd`.

## After the Cycle

Defeating Lucifer rolls credits. The save profile is now in NG+ eligible state. Returning to Babilim and entering the Ascension Plane lets the player begin a new cycle. Mobs are 2x stronger, drops are 2x more frequent, the world feels heavier. Permanent unlocks (Demon, Sun Breathing) carry. Run state (Tiamat alive, Lucifer alive, all quests reset) restarts.

The Storyteller in the Ascension Plane has different lines for each cycle. She has been waiting for you. She has been waiting for you each time before, too.
